Paneer Butter Masala

Delicious North Indian Curry Recipe

Paneer Butter Masala (also known as Butter Paneer) is a rich and creamy curry made with soft paneer cubes cooked in a smooth tomato-based gravy. It’s one of the most popular dishes in Indian restaurants, often served with naan or rice. The gravy is mildly spiced, slightly sweet, and full of buttery goodness.

Recipe Details

Preparation Time Cooking Time Total Time Servings Difficulty
15 minutes 25 minutes 40 minutes 2-3 people Easy

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer (cottage cheese), cut into cubes
  • 2 ripe tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon cream
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon cashew paste (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Preparation Steps:

  1. Chop onions and tomatoes finely or blend them into a smooth puree.
  2. Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Stir in chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  6. Add cashew paste and ¼ cup of water for a creamy gravy texture.
  7. Add paneer cubes and simmer for 5–7 minutes on low heat.
  8. Mix in the cream and stir gently.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander and a small cube of butter before serving.

Serving Suggestion:

Paneer Butter Masala tastes best when served hot with:

  • Butter naan
  • Jeera rice
  • Plain roti or chapati
  • Garlic naan or paratha

Nutrition Facts (Approx. per serving):

  • Calories: 320 kcal
  • Protein: 12 g
  • Fat: 24 g
  • Carbohydrates: 14 g
  • Fiber: 3 g

Chef’s Tips:

  • Use fresh paneer for a soft and creamy texture.
  • Soak paneer cubes in warm water for 10 minutes to make them extra soft.
  • For restaurant-style flavor, add a pinch of sugar and a teaspoon of kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves).
  • If you like spicy food, add a slit green chili while cooking the gravy.
  • For a smoky flavor, place a piece of hot charcoal in the pan with ghee for a minute before serving (optional).

Tips & Variations:

  • Make it vegan: Replace paneer with tofu and cream with coconut milk.
  • Add more vegetables like peas or bell peppers for variation.
  • Use cashew or almond paste to make it thicker and richer.
  • Try it with brown rice for a healthier option.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Can I use store-bought paneer?
Yes, but homemade paneer is usually softer. Soak store-bought paneer in warm water before using.
2. How do I make it less spicy?
Use less chili powder and add more cream to balance the spice.
3. Can I freeze Paneer Butter Masala?
Yes, you can freeze the gravy (without paneer) for up to a month. Add fresh paneer before serving.
4. What can I use instead of cream?
You can use milk or coconut milk as a lighter alternative.
5. Can I make this recipe without onion and garlic?
Yes! Skip them and use extra tomatoes and cashew paste for a Jain-friendly version.

Fun Fact:

The famous Paneer Butter Masala originated in Delhi and was inspired by the legendary “Butter Chicken.” It was created as a vegetarian version so that everyone could enjoy the same rich, buttery flavors!
